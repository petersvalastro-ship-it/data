# Unit at a glance — all bands

## Essential question

*How can I understand and care for my brain—especially when I use technology?*

## Through-line

The brain is a **blueprint** we shape with **habits**, **rest**, **relationships**, and **intentional tech use**—like architects plan structures that are strong, safe, and good for the people inside.

## Shared lesson arc (adjust days per band)

| Phase | Focus | Outcome |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | **Blueprint** — what the brain does | Students name major functions; connect to experience |
| 2 | **Wiring** — messages, attention, feelings | Simple (3–4) → technical (7–8) models |
| 3 | **Tech on the brain** — tools, design, time | Ethical, non-shaming framing |
| 4 | **Neural audit** — data about my week | Honest self-study; class norms |
| 5 | **Master builder** — create with tech | Tie anatomy / cognition to **making** |
| 6 | **Culmination** — poster, talk, or project | Public “architect” statement |

## Band summaries

| Band | Cognitive load | Culmination examples |
|------|----------------|----------------------|
| **3–4** | Concrete, story-based, minimal jargon | Poster, class charter, simple build |
| **5–6** | Introduce core vocabulary; short research | Slides, podcast, annotated diagram |
| **7–8** | Executive function, reward systems (careful), ethics | Research brief, policy proposal, mini-documentary |

## Non-negotiables (all grades)

- **No shame** about screen use; **agency** and **choice architecture**.
- **Privacy:** never require students to disclose sensitive home details; offer fictional examples.
- **Not medical advice**; escalate mood/sleep concerns to families and professionals per school policy.
