# Answer key — Grades 7–8

## 2 — Models

**Misconception:** The brain is **highly active** across regions; “10%” is a myth. Accept nuanced answers about **efficiency** vs **unused mass**.

## 3 — Executive function

**Scenario:** Working memory split between task and phone; inhibitory control tempted by notifications; flexibility reduced under split attention.  
**Environmental changes:** phone away, full-screen focus, breaks scheduled, etc.

## 4–5

Open-ended; reward **accurate mechanism language** without overclaiming.

## 6 — Reward / prediction

**Sample idea:** Unpredictable rewards **violate expectations** and trigger **prediction updates** (learning signals); feeds exploit **novelty** and **variable timing**.  
**Strategy examples:** remove badge counts, batch checks, grayscale, app limits, replace habit loop.

## 7–8

Discussion keys: **attention sold to advertisers**; design patterns are **intentional**; privacy is **partially** user-configurable but **not** only individual responsibility.

## 9 — Neural audit

No numeric key; use for **inquiry** and **experiment design**.

## 11 — Research

Check: **multiple sources**, **credibility criteria**, **claim vs evidence**, **acknowledged limitations**.

Use `teacher-guide/assessment-rubric.md` + writing standards.
