# Answer key — Grades 3–4

> Many items are **open-ended**; sample acceptable responses guide scoring.

## A — My amazing brain

4. **True** — brains change with practice, sleep, and supportive environments (neuroplasticity in simple terms).

## B — Brain jobs

Accept any **reasonable** mapping; examples:

- Remembered lunch → **remember**  
- Breath when upset → **feel** (and self-regulation)  
- Balance → **move**

## C — Pathway story

Correct order: **1 see → 2 understand → 3 feel happy → 4 wave back** (3 and 2 may be debated; accept if student explains *thinking before feeling*).

## D — SEL

No single answer; look for **specific strategy** + **appropriate adult** if needed.

## E — Tool or trap

**Intended** (discuss nuance in class):

1. Often **Tool** if bounded; **Trap** if endless—either OK with justification.  
2. Often **Trap** (time loss, fatigue).  
3. **Tool** (creation).  
4. Often **Trap** for sleep (or **Trap** pattern if habitual).

## F — Neural audit

No key; use for **conversation** and **goal-setting**.

## G — Master builder

Rubric: **plan clarity**, **safety**, **connection to unit ideas**.

## H — Wellness blueprint

Check for **specific, doable** rules (not “never use iPad” unless family-driven).

## I — Wonder log

Credit **genuine questions** and **one reasonable source type**.
