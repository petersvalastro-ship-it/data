# Teacher guide — overview

## Purpose

**Brain Blueprint: Architects of the Mind** helps students see themselves as **designers** of attention, emotional regulation, and technology habits—grounded in **how brains learn** and **how designed systems interact with attention**.

## Materials you need

- This workbook (student PDF or printouts per band)  
- Your **Brain Diagram** study guide and worksheets (`Study Guide Brain Diagram`, `Brain Diagram Worksheets`)  
- **SEL–brain** anchor (`Connect SEL to the Brain`)  
- **Neural Audit** PDF as reference or direct handout (`Neural Audit Screen Time Application`)  
- Optional: **Master Builder’s Brain Lab**, **Link Brain Anatomy to Creative Tech Use**, **Brain Science of Tech** / **Research Phase 1** for extensions  
- Video hook: `Architects_of_the_Mind.mp4`  
- **Blueprint for Digital Wellness** image as exemplar

## Pedagogy notes

1. **Model curiosity** about your own phone habits (appropriately); avoid hypocrisy triggers.  
2. **Co-create norms** before audits (“this is for learning, not grading character”).  
3. **Differentiate** the culminating task (poster vs podcast vs research) within each band.  
4. **Partner with families** for home screen norms—send a one-page “what we did” summary.

## Formative assessment

- Exit tickets in each `student-workbook.md`  
- Neural audit (before/after optional)  
- Culminating rubric: use `assessment-rubric.md` and merge with your existing `Architects of the Mind Assessment` + answer key PDF

## Time (suggested)

| Band | Minimum | Comfortable |

| 5–6 | 6 × 40–45 min | 10–12 sessions |
| 7–8 | 8 × 45–50 min | 12–15 sessions + project |
