# Pacing and standards touchpoints

> Align wording with **your** state framework; below are **common anchor ideas**, not exhaustive claims.

## Grades 3–4 — sample pacing (7 sessions × ~35 min)

| Day | Topic | Student workbook section |
|-----|--------|---------------------------|
| 1 | Unit launch + video hook | Cover, Unit Promise |
| 2 | Brain as blueprint | A, B |
| 3 | Messages pathway | C |
| 4 | SEL + brain | D |
| 5 | Tool vs trap | E |
| 6 | Neural audit + wellness blueprint | F, H |
| 7 | Master builder + poster | G, Culmination |

**Standards touchpoints:** health / body systems (brain’s role in learning); SEL (self-awareness, self-management); responsible technology use.

## Grades 5–6 — sample pacing (9 sessions × ~40 min)

| Day | Topic | Student workbook section |
|-----|--------|---------------------------|
| 1–2 | Launch + vocabulary + diagram deep dive | 1–3 |
| 3 | Neurons & synapses (metaphor) | 4 |
| 4 | Attention & habits | 5 |
| 5 | SEL regulation science-lite | 6 |
| 6 | Tech design: tool vs trap + ethics seed | 7 |
| 7 | Neural audit + goal | 8, 9 |
| 8 | Master builder | 10 |
| 9 | Culmination + exit | 11–12 |

**Standards touchpoints:** NGSS-style practices (models, evidence); health; digital citizenship; speaking/listening if presenting.

## Grades 7–8 — sample pacing (12+ sessions)

Embed **research** and **ethics** case; longer **executive function** and **reward learning** thread. See `grades-7-8/student-workbook.md` section map in its header.

**Standards touchpoints:** research skills; argument from evidence; digital literacy; ethical reasoning; health.

## Cross-band formative checklist

- [ ] Can the student name **one** brain function in their own words?  
- [ ] Can they describe **one** strategy when feelings are big?  
- [ ] Can they distinguish **tool-like** vs **trap-like** tech patterns **without shame**?  
- [ ] Can they state **one** measurable habit change for the week?
